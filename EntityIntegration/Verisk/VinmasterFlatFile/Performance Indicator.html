Performance information in the VINMASTER® Performance Indicator field (position 63) is purely informational
40
in nature, and companies must secure regulatory approval, where necessary, in order to incorporate such
41
information into their rating program. These performance classes were never part of the ISO Symbol and
42
Identification Manual and are derived by formulas detailed below:

45
blankStandardAny non-sports vehicle with a net weight to horsepower ratio of greater than 16 to 1.

1IntermediateAny non-sports vehicle with a net weight to horsepower ratio of 16 to 1 or less, but greater than 14 to 1.
47
2HighAny non-sports vehicle with a net weight to horsepower ratio of 14 to 1 or less.
48
3SportsAll vehicle series for which only a two-passenger body type is manufactured and which have a net weight to horsepower ratio less than or equal to 30 to 1 but greater than 20 to 1. Sports Group does not include station wagon, sedan, pickup, utility or van types. If a two-plus-two version of a two-passenger body type is also manufactured and the two-plus-two version also meets the net weight to horsepower qualification, the two-plus-two will be included in the Sports Group.

4Sports PremiumAll vehicle series for which only a two-passenger body type is manufactured and which have a net weight to horsepower ratio less than or equal to 20 to 1. Sports Premium Group does not include station wagon, sedan, pickup, utility or van types. If a two-plus-two version of a two-passenger body type is also manufactured and the two-plus-two version also meets the net weight to horsepower qualification, the two-plus-two will be included in the Sports Premium Group.

IMPORTANT: There is one caveat to using the VINMASTER® Performance designation. Had we continued the
52
performance program, which was designed in the “muscle-car” era, it would have filed a revision to account
53
for the fact that strictly using weight-to-horsepower ratios with today’s cars will sometimes yield an unintended
54
result. For example, a GMC pickup is not a performance vehicle but certain models may meet the performance
55
criteria strictly using weight-to-horsepower ratios. So, some additional logic or judgment would have to be
56
put in place to fine tune the results.
