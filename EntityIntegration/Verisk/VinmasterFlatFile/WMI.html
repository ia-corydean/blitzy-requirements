The World Manufacturer Identifier (positions 1 - 3 of the VIN) defines the Country of
4
Origin, Manufacturer and Vehicle Type (and sometimes Make) of the vehicle. The
5
World Manufacturer Identifier is a three-digit, alphanumeric code (e.g. JH4 = Honda
6
Motor Co. LTD., Japan - Acura Passenger Car). Please note: There have been a few
7
instances where different manufacturers have used the same codes for the first three
8
positions of the VIN.
9
10
Prior to the implementation of a standard VIN format in 1981, the WMI was a two digit
11
numeric code (listed in the ISO Personal Automobile Statistical Plan). When only two
12
digits (e.g. 21 = Chevrolet), the WMI is located in position 1 and 2 and position 3 is blank.
13
For a list of pre-standard WMIs, please refer to the "Pre Standard WMI Codes" table
14
below. For Model Year 1981 vehicles and subsequent, the WMI is a three digit, alpha-
15
numeric code (e.g. 1G1 = Chevrolet).
16
17
For pre 1981 vehicles, the remainder of the VIN is variable in length up to a maximum of a
18
seven digit alphanumeric code. For 1981 and subsequent, the remainder of the VIN is a
19
uniform length of seven characters.
